Line Pairs + Lens Blur = Reduced Contrast

A line-pair object can be represented as a square wave: bright, dark, bright, dark. After passing through a lens or imaging system, sharp edges become blurred. The image profile becomes smoother, more like a sine wave, and the contrast decreases. Higher spatial frequencies are affected more strongly.
